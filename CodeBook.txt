	The above variables #3 to #68 are all derived from smartphone accelerometer or gyroscope signals, related to time domain signals in X, Y, Z directions
	These variables are the mean measurement for a given activity and subject, based on the raw data from UCI HAR dataset.

	The format for the variables names are standardized by the following way:
	first letter: t or f describes data in either time (t) or frequency (f) domain.
	second word: Body (or BodyBody) or Gravity describes separation of acceleration by either the body (Body) or gravity (Gravity)
	Third word: Acc or Gyro describes whether the signal is from the accelerometer (Acc) or gyroscope (Gyro) from the smartphone
	Fourth word (optional): Jerk describes further signals describing jerk signals derived from linear acceleration and angulary velocity (Jerk)
	Fifth word (optional): Mag describes the total magnitude (not X, Y, Z components) of each signal
	Sixth word: Mean or Std describes whether the mean of this dataset is taken from the mean (Mean) or standard deviation (Std) of the original UCI HAR dataset.
	Seventh word (optional): X, Y, Z describes which axis of measurement. Any measurement without X, Y or Z should have a magnitude (Mag) associated instead.
